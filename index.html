Pilot Training

Being a pilot takes hard work, focus, and determination. You'll learn everything about aviation, aircraft, and navigation. It would be best if you were in good shape, both physically and mentally. Training includes classes, simulations, and actual flying. Your job is to operate aircraft safely and make wise decisions.

Aerospace Engineering

Aerospace engineering involves designing aircraft and spacecraft and requires aerodynamics, materials science, and propulsion systems knowledge. Engineers are developing advanced materials to reduce weight and improve efficiency and new engines to reduce emissions and improve fuel efficiency.

Learn More
Aviation Mechanic

As an aviation mechanic, your job is to keep aircraft safe and operational. You inspect and fix mechanical and electrical components and prioritize safety by addressing potential issues before takeoff. Good communication and problem-solving skills are essential in working with pilots and other professionals.
